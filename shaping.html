What is Shaping?

Shaping occurs when you break one behavior down into its composite steps. You click and reward individual elements or stages of a single behavior until the cat can perform all of them fluidly. For instance, many cats are fearful of the carrier, and will not approach it. You can use shaping to train the cat to enter his carrier gradually. Click and reward for any movement toward the carrier. Continue to click and reward for each step that takes the cat closer to the carrier until he reaches the end goal of completely entering the carrier on cue. A useful tool when shaping a behavior is a shaping plan. Gabe Kahsen provides a nice overview video of creating a training plan for cats in his video How to Write a Shaping Plan | Cat Training.

Helpful Hints

  • Don't wait for the "whole picture" or the perfect behavior. Click and treat for small movements in the right direction. You want the cat to sit, and he starts to crouch in back: click. You want the cat to come when called, and she takes a few steps your way: click.
  • Keep raising your goal. As soon as you have a good response-when a cat, for example, goes to place repeatedly-start asking for more. Wait a few beats, until the cat stays in place a little longer, then click.
  • Above all, have fun. Clicker training is a wonderful way to enrich your relationship with your cat.
