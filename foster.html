Foster Resources

Thank you for being a PAWS foster! We couldn't achieve our mission without the homes, love, and care you provide!

As a foster, you have an everchanging group of cats to care for. Each brings their own set of experiences, challenges, and adorable behaviors! Being in a home provides the enviroment many need while healing or when shelter life becomes too stressful, but can also create some stressful situations.

The resources on this page will help you provide enrichment and assist you and your foster cat with functional behaviors.

Carrier Training

Carriers are scary for most cats. Many associate a carrier with something unpleasant, but it doesn't have to be a negative experience. In this video, Erin Mayes, PAWS Feline Behavorist, explains how to use clicker training to make carriers less scary.

High Five

One of the easiest tricks to teach a cat is to give a high five. Using a target stick to capture their attention and transitioning to your hand creates adorable skills that will get cats adopted! Check out the Cat Pawsitive: Home Edition video on training your foster to give high fives!

Clicker Training Shy Cats

Shy or fearful cats are often overlooked and spend more time in shelters. As a foster, you can help these cats overcome their fear of humans through positive reinforcement and socialization. Creating situatios where good things happen to the cat when they interact with you will build their comfort and confidence around humans. Best Friends Animal Society's article How to Socialize Very Shy or Fearful Cats offers some great tips.

Socializing Kittens

A primary goal of PAWS is to create a no-kill Chicago. A key component of that goal is spaying and neutering colony cats as well as pets. Each year hundreds of kittens are born, many to colony cats who have limited relationships with humans. We are fortunate to rescue many of these kittens before they learn to fear humans. These rescued kittens may be shy and will need socializing to get them adoption ready. The article How to Use Food to Socialize Kittens offers tips to make that happen.

Promoting Your Foster

As a foster, social media is a great tool to promote your foster cat and a great way to encourage others to become foster families! Check out the PAWS Chicago Social Media for Fosters page for ideas on how to promote your foster!
