What is Luring?

Luring is a technique that requires the trainer to use reward items to direct the cat into the desired behavior. The reward guides the cat, and once the behavior is complete, the handler immediately clicks and then rewards.

Fading the Lure

Before putting new behaviors on cue, the handler must “fade the lure.” To fade the lure, continue to use your hand to guide the cat into the desired behavior, but do so without holding the reward.

Once your cat is consistently performing the desired behavior, the hand gesture used to guide the cat can become a non-verbal cue for the behavior. You can also replace the non-verbal cue with a verbal cue.

Changing a Cue

If you opt to change the cue, begin by uttering or using the new cue and follow it with the known non-verbal cue. Click and reward as soon as the cat performs the desired behavior. Over time, you can increase the length of time between the new cue and the older cue. Once the cat begins performing the behavior prior to introducing the old cue, you can then start to fade the old cue out and only click and reward after presenting the new cue to the cat.

Helpful Hints

  • Click for voluntary (or accidental) movements toward your goal. You may coax or lure the animal into a movement or position, but don't push, pull, or hold it. Let the animal discover how to do the behavior on its own.
  • Fading the lure is a crucial step that should be done early to avoid the cat only performing the behavior with a lure. After the first 5 repetitions of a behavior with the lure, remove the lure and repleat the same hand motion. Continue practicing without the lure before adding any verbal cue.
