What is Targeting?

Targeting requires the cat to touch a part of his body (usually his nose, head, or paw) to a specific object. We use target sticks with this technique, but you can also use your finger or even a pencil as your target.

Begin by presenting the target to your cat. Place the target within a few inches of the cat’s face. Many cats will naturally sniff the end of the target to investigate it. When this happens, immediately click and reward. Continue doing this until the cat reliably sniffs or touches the target when it’s presented. As your cat becomes more adept at touching the target, increase the distance between the cat and the target.

When you are ready to put the behavior on cue, utter your verbal cue (target and touch are common verbal cues for this behavior). As the cat makes contact with the target, immediately click, and then reward. Eventually, you should be able to utter the cue just before presenting the target. Once the cat touches the target, click, and reward.

Targeting is an excellent technique that serves as the foundation for many other behaviors, including high-five, go to place, jump, and fist bump to name just a few.

Helpful Hints

  • If you are working with a shy cat, use an adjustable target stick to provide them interaction at a distance.
  • Most cats will naturally sniff at a target stick. If you cat doesn't, try putting a bit of wet food or treat on the end of the target to get their attention.
