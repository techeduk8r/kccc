ABOUT KCCC

In January of 2020 PAWS Chicago was accepted into the Jackson Galaxy Project Cat Pawsitive clicker training program. A team of volunteers began learning about clicker training and how it could enrich the lives of some of our longer-term feline residents. This training program was cut short by the COVID pandemic but had generated interest in providing this type of enrichment.

Beginning in fall of 2020, the Jackson Galaxy Foundation opened a new round of clicker training facility applications. PAWS jumped at the chance to participate and was excited to be selected for the advanced version of the program, Cat Pawsitive Pro.

Led by Erin Mayes, Associate Director Feline Behavior, a new team of PAWS volunteers began clicker training. Over the course of four months, a team of six volunteers worked with Erin to learn core training techniques and provide enrichment for six cats who had been with PAWS for an extended period of time.

The cats selected to participate were Mandy, Nadia, Sweater, Ryker, and Rudy Rudy. Later in the training Tad joined in on the fun. Each of these cats had unique needs for enrichment with most needing more mental and physical stimulation. Twice a day for four to five days a week these cats worked with our volunteer trainers on skills such as high-five, sit, hoop jumps, spin, place and go to crate. These skills were fun and functional, making each cat easier for medical staff to interact with and increased their adoptability.

Upon the conclusion of the Cat Pawsitive Pro, Erin wanted to keep the momentum going by designing a permanent program to provide clicker training and enrichment for PAWS cats. Together with Cat Pawsitive Pro team leader, Briana Allen, Erin developed training materials, instructional videos, and a data collection process that became Erinâ€™s dream program, Kitty City Clicker Club!

The Kitty City Clicker Club is open to all Kitty City volunteers. Volunteers will participate in a workshop on training techniques, the program, and its processes and resources. Workshops will run quarterly and new cats will be added to the program on a rolling basis. Kitty City volunteers interested in learning more about the Kitty City Clicker Club can visit Erin the next time they are at LAPC or email her.
